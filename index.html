Quotes I was told

"Love is the most important thing in the world, but baseball is pretty good, too" "In baseball, as in life, persistence pays off" "Dream big, work hard, and let your love for the game guide you"

These quotes were told to me and they helped me throuout my baseball career. I always remebred the quotes when I was down and out.

Baseball by a tree

One of my favorite pictures of a baseball by a tree. It gived off childhood memories of the area I grew up in, swinging the bat while my dad would toss the balls to me.

The Baseball Diamond

The Diamond was my home away from home, holding many memories with my friends I will never forget. Everytime I drive past my old high school baseball field I reflect on all the good and bad times at practice my friends and I went through.

The Dugout

The Dugout has a special place for me as my friends and I would mess around before and after games

How Long I've Been Playing

I started playing the game at the age of 4 years old. I started in tee-ball all the way to my senior year of high school.

What does this sport mean to me

Baseball was a blessing in disguise. I faced a lot of challenges when it came to this sport. Whether it was losing by a comeback or how to cope with unfair treatment. But in the end I learned to grow and understand how the real world works.

What position did I play?

In little league and middle school I was a pitcher and an outfielder. But once I started high school baseball the coaches wanted me for my speed and my hitting so I moved between a left fielder and a designated hitter.

What my favorite memory was

My favorite memory was when I got the oppurutnity to start on varsity which led me and the team to go on a winning streak.

Who is my favorite baseball player?

Although my favorite baseball player changed from time to time, the one player I loved seeing the most was Trea Turner. Him and I both had speed and I mimicked his swinging stance.

What is my favorite baseball movie?

My favorite baseball movie is Moneyball. Because it tell the story of a real life underdog story of the Oakland Athletics. It tells the compelling story of how the team was able to compete with big market teams with a small budget.
